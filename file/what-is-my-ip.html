What Is My IP Address?

A Simple Guide to Finding Your Public IP and What It Means

Published: September 11, 2025 | Reading Time: 4 minutes

Your "IP address" is your unique address on the internet. Finding it is incredibly easy. This guide will show you how and explain what this information means.

The Easiest Way to Find Your IP

You don't need to look through any complicated settings on your computer or phone. The simplest way to find your public IP address is to visit a website that tells you what it is.

Your Public IP Address Is:

To find out, simply visit a tool like iplogger.icu. The moment you load the page, your IP address will be displayed prominently at the top.

It will look something like this:

8.8.8.8

Public vs. Private IP Address

You actually have two IP addresses!

  • Public IP: This is the main one, the address the world sees. It's the one used by websites and online services. This is the IP you find using an online tool.
  • Private IP: This is the local address your router assigns to devices inside your home network. It usually looks like 192.168.x.x. It's not visible to the outside world.
When people talk about "finding an IP," they almost always mean the public IP address.

What Does Your IP Address Reveal?

Your public IP address provides some general information about you, but it's not as scary as it sounds. Here’s what someone can learn from it:

  • Your Geographic Location: Your country, state, and city. Not your street address.
  • Your Internet Service Provider (ISP): The company you pay for internet (e.g., Comcast, Verizon).
  • Your Name or Identity: Your personal information is kept private by your ISP.
  • Your Exact Home Address: The location is only accurate to the city level.
