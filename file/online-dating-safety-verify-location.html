Online Dating Safety Guide

How to Verify Someone's Real Location Before Meeting

Protect yourself from catfish, scammers, and dangerous situations

Why Location Verification Matters

Online dating can be amazing, but it's important to verify who you're really talking to before meeting in person.

Catfish Protection

Make sure they're really who they say they are and where they claim to be located.

Scammer Detection

Avoid romance scammers who pretend to be local but are actually overseas.

Distance Verification

Confirm they're actually within dating distance and not lying about proximity.

Major Red Flags to Watch For

Won't Video Chat

Always has excuses for not doing video calls

Won't Talk on Phone

Only wants to text, never calls or voice messages

Avoids Meeting

Always has last-minute cancellations or excuses

Asks for Money

Emergency situations, travel money, or "loans"

Vague About Location

Can't give specific details about local places

Limited Photos

Very few photos or refuses to send new ones

How to Verify Their Real Location

1

The Casual Link Method

This is the easiest and most natural way to verify someone's location:

  • Go to iplogger.icu and create a tracking link
  • Use something interesting like a funny video, news article, or meme
  • Send it casually: "This made me think of you!" or "You have to see this!"
  • When they click, you'll see their real location
Pro Tip:

Send content that matches their interests. If they love cats, send a cat video. If they're into sports, send a sports article.

2

The Local Knowledge Test

Ask specific questions about their claimed location:

  • "What's your favorite restaurant near [specific landmark]?"
  • "How's the traffic on [local highway] during rush hour?"
  • "Did you hear about [recent local news event]?"
  • "Which [local store/mall] do you usually go to?"

Watch for: Vague answers, changing the subject, or responses that could apply to any city.

3

Request Location-Specific Photos

Ask for photos that prove they're in the claimed location:

  • "Can you send a selfie in front of [local landmark]?"
  • "What's the weather like? Send me a pic of outside!"
  • "I'm curious about your neighborhood, any photos?"
  • "Show me your favorite local coffee shop!"
4

Check Social Media Consistency

Look for clues on their social media profiles:

  • Check-ins and tagged locations
  • Friends and their locations
  • Background details in photos
  • Timezone consistency in posts

Additional Safety Measures

Before Meeting in Person:

  • Always do a video call first
  • Have multiple phone conversations
  • Verify their location multiple times
  • Do a reverse image search on their photos
  • Tell friends where you're going

During First Meetings:

  • Meet in public places only
  • Drive yourself or use rideshare
  • Keep first dates short
  • Keep your phone charged
  • Share your live location with friends

When to Walk Away

Immediate Red Flags - End Communication

  • Location tracking shows they're in a different country
  • Refuses all forms of verification
  • Asks for money, gifts, or personal information
  • Stories keep changing
  • Won't video chat after several requests
  • Uses stolen photos (reverse image search)
  • Grammar/language doesn't match claimed location
  • Your gut feeling says something is wrong
