Beginner's Guide IP Tracking

First, What is an IP Address?

Imagine every device connected to the internet has a unique mailing address. That's basically what an IP address is. It's a special number that identifies a device on the internet, like a computer, phone, or tablet.

While it won't show you someone's exact house, it can tell you some interesting things, like:

  • The country they are in.
  • The city or region where they are located.
  • The company that provides their internet (their ISP).

This guide will show you the easiest way to find this information, even if you're a complete beginner.

Why Might You Want to Find an IP Address?

People do this for many simple reasons:

  • Curiosity: Wondering where an online friend or gamer is really from.
  • Safety: Checking if someone you met online is being honest about their location.
  • Business: Understanding where your website visitors are coming from.
  • Preventing Scams: Identifying the location of a suspicious email sender.

The 3-Step Guide to Finding an IP Address

1

Create a Special Link

First, you need a link to something interesting on the internet. This could be a news article, a funny YouTube video, a blog post, or even an image. Copy the URL of that page.

Now, go to a free and easy tool like iplogger.icu. Paste your link into the box on their homepage and click the button to create your special tracking link.

2

Share the Link

Now you have a new, shorter link. This is your magic link! Send it to the person whose IP you want to find. You can send it through a chat message, email, or social media post.

Tip: Say something natural, like "Hey, check out this crazy video!" or "You won't believe this news story." When they click the link, they will be taken to the original article or video you chose, so they won't suspect anything.

3

Check the Results!

Go back to the IPLogger website. In the "Logged IPs" section, you will see a new entry every time someone clicks your link. It's that simple!

You will see their IP address, their approximate location (country and city), the type of device they used, and more. You've successfully found their IP address! For a full breakdown of what this information means, check out our guide on what you can find with an IP address.

Understanding the Information You Get

Here's a simple breakdown of what you'll see:

  • IP Address: The unique number of their device.
  • Country: The country they are in.
  • City: The city or general area.
  • ISP: Their internet provider (e.g., Comcast, Verizon).
  • Device: The type of device (e.g., Windows PC, iPhone).
  • Time: When they clicked the link.

This method works for chats and social media. You can also learn how to track an email's location using a similar technique.
