🔒What is Zero-Width Character Encryption?

Have you ever wanted to send a seemingly ordinary message on WhatsApp, Telegram, or Discord that actually contains a secret message only specific people can see? That's the magic of zero-width character encryption!

Zero-width character encryption is a super "invisible" encryption technique. It uses special characters that are completely invisible to the naked eye (like zero-width spaces) to hide your secret information within normal text. When others see your message, they only see the surface content and have no idea there's a secret hidden inside!

Example:

Hidden message: "I love you"

🧠Technical Principle (Skip if you're not technical)

Zero-width characters include: Zero Width Space (U+200B), Zero Width No-Break Space (U+FEFF), Zero Width Joiner (U+200D), etc. Privnote.chat converts your secret message into binary data, then uses these invisible characters to represent 0s and 1s, finally inserting them into normal text. Decryption reverses this process.

Simply put: Visible text is for everyone to see, invisible text is for those who know how to look. Like invisible ink, but digital!
