🔒What is Zero-Width Character Encryption?

Have you ever thought about sending what looks like an ordinary message on WhatsApp, Telegram, or other chat apps, but actually hiding secret content that only specific people can see? That's the magic of zero-width character encryption!

Zero-width character encryption is a super "invisible" encryption technology. It uses special characters that are completely invisible to the naked eye (like zero-width spaces) to hide your secret information within normal text. When others see your message, they only see the surface content and have no idea there's a secret hidden inside!

🧠Technical Principle (Skip if not tech-savvy)

Zero-width characters include: zero-width space (U+200B), zero-width non-breaking space (U+FEFF), zero-width joiner (U+200D), etc. Privnote.chat converts your secret message to binary data, then uses these invisible characters to represent 0s and 1s, finally inserting them into normal text. Decryption reverses this process.

Simply put: visible text is for others to see, invisible text is for those who know. Like invisible ink, it's magical!
